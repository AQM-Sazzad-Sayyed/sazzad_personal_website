Research Projects

Multi-Stage Training for Activity Recognition

Role: Co-author | Duration: 2019-2020

Developed a novel multi-stage training approach for deep neural networks to recognize human activities from multimodal wearable sensor data. The approach addresses challenges of noisy time-series data and improves recognition accuracy through systematic feature learning stages.

Deep Learning Activity Recognition Sensor Fusion

CLUE: Bringing Machine Unlearning to Mobile Devices

Role: Lead Researcher | Duration: 2025 - 2026

We tackle the challenge of class level unlearning for classification task and introduce the key idea that unlearned classes should appear as OOD to the unlearned model. We consistently improve unlearning performance by upto 4.74% as compared to the SOTA baselines with 68% less power consumption and 90% latency improvement.

ENCORE: A Neural Collapse Perspective on Out-of-Distribution Detection in Deep Neural Networks

Role: Lead Researcher | Duration: 2025

We revisit the prominent energy based OOD score through the lens of neural collapse. This reveals that uniform feature scaling improves the ID-OOD separability. Based on the theory of Neural collapse, our redesigned energy score improves over 6% relative to the closest SOTA.

View Paper Github
OOD Detection Neural Collapse

DARTS: Distance-Aware Robust Training for Selective Classification

Role: Lead Researcher | Duration: 2025

DARTS is a geometry-aware selective classification method that improves reliability by explicitly shaping feature-space representations using a closed-form distance-to-decision-boundary formulation. It enhances confidence separation and robustness without extra parameters or inference cost, achieving up to 58% AURC improvement across CIFAR and ImageNet benchmarks.
