PhD Student in Electrical and Computer Engineering

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Northeastern University, specializing in machine learning, deep learning, and trustworthy AI. My research focuses on out-of-distribution detection, uncertainty estimation, selective classification, and robust representation learning, with an emphasis on developing reliable and efficient deep learning systems for safety-critical applications.

My work combines theoretical analysis with practical system design, spanning areas such as computer vision, multimodal learning, anomaly detection, and machine unlearning. I am particularly interested in understanding the geometry and uncertainty characteristics of deep neural networks, including feature-space structure, decision boundary behavior, and representation alignment in modern architectures such as Vision Transformers and convolutional neural networks.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I earned my degree in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology, where I built a strong foundation in signal processing, communications, and applied mathematics. I have experience working with large-scale deep learning systems using PyTorch and related frameworks, and I actively contribute to research at the intersection of AI reliability, robustness, and real-world deployment.

My research has resulted in publications and ongoing work in areas including OOD detection, uncertainty-aware learning, adversarial robustness, and information fusion. I am passionate about developing machine learning systems that are not only accurate, but also interpretable, efficient, and dependable under distribution shifts and uncertain environments.

I am currently seeking research internship and full-time opportunities in machine learning, computer vision, and AI systems, where I can contribute to advancing robust and trustworthy artificial intelligence.
